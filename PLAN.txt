Section 1
Integrated Institutional Attendance, Presence Verification and Examination Movement Management System

Executive Summary

This project aims to develop a comprehensive institutional attendance and presence verification platform that serves students, academic staff, non-academic staff, faculty administrators, and institutional management.

The platform will provide secure, location-aware, biometric attendance verification for:

	•	Classroom Attendance
	•	Seminar Attendance
	•	Chapel/Mass Attendance
	•	Staff Attendance
	•	Special Institutional Events
	•	Examination Leave and Movement Tracking

The system shall eliminate attendance fraud, proxy attendance, location spoofing, and unauthorized attendance submissions while maintaining a seamless experience for users.

The solution shall operate through:

	1.	Dedicated Attendance Terminals installed at approved venues.
	2.	Mobile Applications for Students and Staff.
	3.	Web Administration Portal.
	4.	Central Laravel-Based Backend Services.
	5.	Offline Synchronization Capabilities.
	6.	Facial Recognition and Fingerprint Verification.
	7.	Venue Authentication Infrastructure.

The system must remain operational even when internet connectivity is unavailable.

⸻

Project Objectives

The system shall ensure:

Identity Verification

Only the actual registered student or staff member can submit attendance.

Venue Verification

Attendance can only be submitted from approved locations.

Time Verification

Attendance can only be submitted during approved attendance windows.

Event Verification

Attendance must correspond to a scheduled class, seminar, mass, staff duty, or institutional event.

Anti-Proxy Enforcement

No individual shall be able to submit attendance on behalf of another person except authorized personnel through approved override mechanisms.

Offline Availability

Attendance activities must continue during network outages and synchronize automatically when connectivity is restored.

Module 8: Institutional Debt Recovery and Financial Compliance Management

Description

The system shall include a comprehensive Debt Recovery Management module responsible for monitoring, accumulating, enforcing, and recovering all financial penalties and outstanding institutional obligations for students.

Rather than treating attendance as an isolated activity, every attendance-related penalty shall automatically become part of the student’s institutional financial record.

The module shall integrate seamlessly with:

	•	School Fees Management
	•	Exeat Management
	•	Attendance Management
	•	Chapel/Mass Attendance
	•	Examination Clearance
	•	Electronic Examination Card
	•	Student Notification System

⸻

Automatic Debt Generation

The system shall automatically generate debts whenever a student violates institutional policies that attract financial penalties.

Examples include:

	•	Missing compulsory chapel or mass
	•	Missing compulsory institutional events
	•	Missing departmental seminars
	•	Late attendance penalties
	•	Exeat violations
	•	Other institution-defined penalty events

Each debt record shall contain:

	•	Student ID
	•	Student Name
	•	Department
	•	Faculty
	•	Session
	•	Semester
	•	Event
	•	Date
	•	Reason for Penalty
	•	Amount
	•	Due Date
	•	Payment Status
	•	Clearance Status

Every generated debt shall immediately appear in the student’s financial dashboard.

⸻

Debt Accumulation

The system shall maintain a cumulative debt ledger for every student.

Instead of viewing debts individually, the platform shall continuously calculate:

	•	Total Outstanding Debt
	•	Paid Debt
	•	Pending Debt
	•	Overdue Debt
	•	Historical Debt Records

This cumulative balance shall be used throughout the institution whenever eligibility decisions are made.

Section 3

Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

Overview

The system shall implement a comprehensive Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) framework to ensure that every user accesses only the features and information relevant to their assigned responsibilities.

Rather than providing separate applications or dedicated portals, the system shall operate as a single integrated platform where permissions are dynamically granted according to institutional roles.

Each authenticated user shall only have access to modules, records, reports, and administrative functions authorized for their role.

Supported Roles

The system shall support configurable roles, including but not limited to:

	•	System Administrator
	•	Quality Assurance (QA)
	•	Debt Recovery Officer
	•	Bursary Officer
	•	Academic Affairs
	•	Registrar
	•	Dean
	•	Head of Department
	•	Programme Coordinator
	•	Lecturer
	•	Examination Officer
	•	Security Personnel
	•	Student Affairs Officer
	•	Staff Member
	•	Student

Institutions shall be able to create additional roles and customize permissions without modifying the application.

Biometric Examination Hall Access Control

The system shall support biometric verification at examination venues to automate student admission into examination halls.

Each examination hall shall be equipped with an approved biometric attendance device capable of verifying student identity using fingerprints or other institution-approved biometric methods.

Upon biometric verification, the system shall instantly retrieve the student’s examination eligibility for the specific course scheduled at that time.

The verification engine shall automatically evaluate:

	•	Student identity.
	•	Registered course.
	•	Scheduled examination.
	•	Attendance eligibility.
	•	Financial clearance.
	•	Institutional compliance.
	•	Any disciplinary restrictions.
	•	Other examination requirements established by the institution.

Qualified Students

Where all eligibility requirements are satisfied, the system shall automatically:

	•	Authenticate the student’s identity.
	•	Record examination attendance.
	•	Register the student’s entry time.
	•	Grant access for the specific examination.

No manual intervention shall be required.

Students Who Do Not Qualify

Where any eligibility requirement has not been met, the biometric device shall deny examination access and display an appropriate status indicating that the student is Not Qualified.

The system shall simultaneously provide the reason(s) for ineligibility, such as:

	•	Insufficient class attendance.
	•	Outstanding school fees.
	•	Outstanding attendance-related debts.
	•	Chapel or Mass attendance deficiency.
	•	Exeat-related obligations.
	•	Missing course registration.
	•	Other institutional restrictions.

Students whose access is denied shall be referred to the Quality Assurance (QA) officers stationed at the examination venue for review, complaint resolution, or escalation in accordance with institutional policy.

Quality Assurance personnel shall serve as the designated exception-management authority, empowered to review exceptional cases, validate supporting documentation, and, where authorized, approve or reject examination access in accordance with established institutional regulations.

⸻

This approach aligns with enterprise systems used by universities and presents the design in a professional Software Requirements Specification (SRS) style. It clearly separates automated system decisions from administrative oversight, with QA acting as an exception handler rather than a manual gatekeeper, while RBAC ensures all functions are managed within a single integrated platform rather than separate portals.

	•	Absence Penalty
	•	Both Late and Absence Penalties

The system shall automatically apply the selected penalties to participants who violate the event attendance policy.

Generated penalties shall immediately become part of the student’s or staff member’s institutional compliance and financial records.

⸻

Event Recurrence

The system shall support multiple scheduling patterns for institutional events, including:

	•	One-Time Events
	•	Daily Events
	•	Weekly Events
	•	Monthly Events
	•	Semester-Based Events
	•	Session-Based Events
	•	Custom Recurring Events

Recurring events shall automatically generate attendance sessions according to the configured schedule without requiring manual recreation.

⸻

Concurrent Event Support

The system shall support multiple events occurring simultaneously across different locations, departments, faculties, or organizational units.

Each event shall maintain its own:

	•	Attendance records
	•	Participant lists
	•	Attendance rules
	•	Penalty configuration
	•	Compliance reports
	•	Notifications
	•	Analytics

Attendance processing for one event shall not interfere with any other active event.

⸻

Automated Event Lifecycle

The system shall automatically manage the complete lifecycle of every event by:

	1.	Activating attendance at the scheduled start time.
	2.	Accepting attendance only within the configured attendance window.
	3.	Classifying attendance as Present, Late, or Absent based on configured rules.
	4.	Closing attendance automatically at the scheduled end time.
	5.	Calculating attendance statistics.
	6.	Applying financial penalties where applicable.
	7.	Updating institutional compliance records.
	8.	Generating reports for authorized users.
	9.	Sending notifications to affected students or staff.
	10.	Updating examination eligibility, debt recovery status, and other dependent institutional processes in real time.

Strategic Recommendations
While the specification is thorough and logically sound, executing a system with this level of cross-departmental enforcement introduces high technical and operational risks. I recommend addressing the following architectural and human bottlenecks:
Technical & Architectural Recommendations
 Implement Strict Biometric Data Privacy (GDPR/NDPR): Storing biometric templates (faces and fingerprints) locally on venue terminals poses a massive security liability. Ensure templates are heavily encrypted using hardware-backed keystores (like Android TEE) and never stored as raw images.  
 Design a Robust Offline Conflict Resolution Matrix: When offline terminals re-sync, timestamps are vulnerable to device clock drift or manipulation. The synchronization engine must use cryptographic handshakes and device-validated logs to resolve conflicts without corrupting the central PostgreSQL ledger.  
 Optimize Database Performance for Peak Loads: At the exact moment an exam starts or a massive chapel session opens, thousands of biometric requests will hit the API concurrently. Leverage Redis heavily not just for caching, but for temporary request throttling and queue buffering to keep PostgreSQL from locking up.  
Operational & Governance Recommendations
 Mitigate "Exam Hall Door" Bottlenecks: Relying purely on biometric gatekeeping right outside an exam hall can cause severe crowding, delays, and student panic if a terminal lags or misidentifies a user. Recommendation: Push the "Pending Clearance" warnings heavily via the Weekly Compliance Notifications so 99% of issues are solved days before the exam.  
 Establish Clear Legal Policies for Staff Deductions: Automatically docking staff salaries based entirely on IoT/terminal attendance data will almost certainly face pushback from labor unions or employment laws. Ensure there is a robust, multi-step manual approval workflow for HR/Deans before Bursary processes salary deductions.  
 Define Clear Grace Periods for Variable Fines: To prevent a flood of students overwhelming the Quality Assurance unit due to false positives (e.g., being marked late due to a terminal queue), build a small operational safety buffer directly into the Event Compliance Engine rules.
